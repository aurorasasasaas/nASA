Cosmic Quest Game Instructions

About the Game

The Cosmic Quest game is designed for 2 to 5 players, ages 8 and up. In this exciting board game, players will navigate through 15 colorful planets inspired by NASA challenges. Each player will draw cards based on the color of the planet they land on, answering questions to progress towards the finish line, "The Sun".

How to Play

The game begins with the "Mom" or instructor, who will have the brochure containing the answers and will judge the players' responses. Players take turns rolling a die to determine how many spaces to move forward. Here’s how the game works:

  1. Each player rolls the die. The player with the highest roll goes first, followed by the others in descending order.
  2. On your turn, roll the die and move forward the number of spaces rolled.
  3. Upon landing on a planet, draw a card corresponding to that color and read the question aloud.
  4. If you answer correctly, you stay on that spot. If you answer incorrectly, you return to your previous position before rolling the die.
  5. The first player to reach "The Sun" wins!

Planet Names

    Planet GJ 581g

    GJ 581g is located in the habitable zone of its star, meaning it’s the right distance for water to exist as a liquid. This planet was chosen because it is one of the first potentially habitable exoplanets discovered!

    Planet HIP 65426

    HIP 65426 is a fascinating exoplanet that orbits a star similar to our Sun. It's unique because it is a gas giant, much like Jupiter, and studying it helps scientists learn about different planetary systems.

    Planet TOI-1518

    TOI-1518 is another intriguing gas giant that has been observed closely. Its atmosphere is different from that of Jupiter, providing insights into how planets form and evolve.

    Planet NY Virginis

    NY Virginis is an exciting discovery in the study of exoplanets. It is a hot Jupiter, which means it's a gas giant that orbits very close to its star. This helps researchers understand extreme planetary climates.

    Planet ITG 15 b

    ITG 15 b is notable for its unique composition. Scientists believe it has a thick atmosphere, which could offer clues about the potential for life on other planets.

    Planet WASP-38 b

    WASP-38 b is a fascinating gas giant located far from Earth. Its discovery has helped improve our understanding of the diversity of planets in our universe.

    Planet EPIC 206032309

    EPIC 206032309 is an important planet for research because it is located in its star’s habitable zone, much like Earth! This makes it a candidate for further exploration.

    Planet KELT-9 b

    KELT-9 b is one of the hottest known exoplanets, with temperatures soaring above 4,300 degrees Fahrenheit. Studying it helps scientists learn about the effects of extreme heat on planetary atmospheres.

    Planet TOI-1221

    TOI-1221 is a unique planet that orbits a bright star. Its features offer insights into the behavior of nearby planetary systems.

    Planet TrES-2b

    TrES-2b is one of the darkest known exoplanets, reflecting less than 1% of the light that hits it. This fascinating quality makes it a target for understanding light absorption in atmospheres.

    Planet MWC 758 c

    MWC 758 c is a giant planet that orbits a young star. Its discovery is important for studying planet formation and the dynamics of young star systems.

    Planet DP Leonis b

    DP Leonis b is a gas giant with intriguing atmospheric characteristics. Its study may provide important insights into atmospheric dynamics in other planetary systems.

    Planet TIC 139270665

    TIC 139270665 is notable for its location in the habitable zone, similar to Earth. It is a significant discovery in the search for potentially habitable planets.
